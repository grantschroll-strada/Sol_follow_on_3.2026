What Sol Health Does

Sol Health is an integrated clinical workforce platform that solves one of mental health care's most stubborn bottlenecks: the broken, unpaid, and fragmented path from graduate education into licensure.

The company recruits therapists-in-training through university partnerships, provides paid clinical hours, expert supervision, and guaranteed caseload — then converts a significant share into revenue-generating associate therapists. The model simultaneously expands the mental health workforce and delivers affordable care to underserved populations.

Employer Adoption of Quality WBL
Sol functions as the employer for both interns and associates, delivering a structured, high-quality WBL experience that is fundamentally superior to the status quo. What Has Changed Since Initial Investment

At the time of Strada's November 2024 investment, Sol operated primarily as an internship platform with limited monetization. The company has since undergone a fundamental shift — evolving into an integrated, revenue-generating clinical workforce platform.

The associate therapist program — clinicians who have graduated but require ~3,000 supervised hours prior to full licensure — has emerged as the primary revenue driver. This evolution reflects a more defined, scalable, and monetizable model than was in place at investment.

On the demand side, Sol has also developed a more segmented approach: nonprofit partnerships drive low-cost intern-level care, while insurance networks (Aetna, Cigna, BCBS, Horizon NJ Medicaid) and Zocdoc power associate-level revenue.

Business Model Deep Dive

Supply — Therapist Pipeline

Interns — Top of Funnel

Sourced through ~65 of ~100 active university partnerships. Paid stipends (~$150/mo), weekly individual + group supervision, caseload guarantee, and licensure prep. 55% express interest in converting to associates.

Associates — Revenue Engine

Pre-licensed clinicians sourced almost entirely from internal pipeline — a structural cost advantage vs. external recruiting. 21 active, 30 committed. Expected unit economics: ~$100K ARR per associate at 30–35% contribution margins.

Supervisors

49 supervisors at ~3:1 ratio. Sol's platform removes administrative burden, allowing supervisors to focus on clinical mentorship rather than logistics.

Demand & Clinical Outcomes

Client Acquisition

Interns: nonprofit + community partnerships → near-zero CAC, sliding scale $0–$30/session. Associates: Zocdoc + major payer networks → standard co-pays ($10–$35), cash-pay $80–$100. Many clients transition with their therapist as they move intern→associate, reducing churn.

Why Margins Are Lower Than Forecast

The shift to an associate-driven model changed the cost structure. Unlike the intern program (near-zero direct cost), associates require therapist compensation, supervision, and client acquisition spend. At maturity, each associate is projected to generate ~$100K in annual revenue at 30–35% contribution margins — a realistic economic model as the business scales. Revenue has largely tracked original underwriting; margin compression reflects deliberate model evolution.

Investment Recommendation

Recommended: Invest $150K Follow-On
Since Strada's initial investment in November 2024, Sol has scaled revenue ~8× and is now tracking in line with original underwriting. The associate therapist program has emerged as a durable, monetizable revenue engine with validated unit economics. While gross margins came in below initial expectations, this reflects a deliberate model evolution — not a reversal of fundamentals. The focus now shifts from proof-of-concept to efficient scaling. Sol continues to be strongly aligned with Strada's focus areas of Work-Based Learning and Affordability, and this follow-on protects Strada's position and increases ownership from 3.57% to 4.64%.
